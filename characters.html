People and Powers

Characters

Track how each figure pressures Gilgamesh to change his understanding of strength, love, and mortality.

Gilgamesh

King of Uruk whose journey moves from abusive power to grief, terror, and partial wisdom.

Enkidu

Wild man, companion, and mirror for Gilgamesh; his death transforms the epic.

Shamhat

Introduces Enkidu to human community, desire, food, clothing, and speech.

Ishtar

Goddess whose rejection by Gilgamesh triggers divine punishment.

Siduri

Tavern keeper who offers practical wisdom about mortal life.

Utnapishtim

Flood survivor who shows Gilgamesh that immortality is exceptional, not a human entitlement.
