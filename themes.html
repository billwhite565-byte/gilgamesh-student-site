Interpretive Lenses

Four Major Themes

Use these lenses to build claims and connect evidence across the whole epic.

1

Friendship Humanizes Power

Enkidu changes Gilgamesh from isolated ruler into someone capable of loyalty, grief, and reflection.

2

Fame Cannot Defeat Death

The heroic quest brings glory, but Enkidu's death exposes the limits of reputation.

3

Civilization Has Costs

Uruk offers meaning and order, but the poem keeps asking what is sacrificed for power and progress.

4

Wisdom Begins with Limits

Gilgamesh matures when he stops trying to escape mortality and learns to see human work differently.
